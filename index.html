Research

My work focuses on UV spectroscopy of planet-hosting stars with the Hubble Space Telescope and sounding rocket experiements. You can find a list of selected publications at the bottom of this page.

Banner image: Artist depiction of the evaporating atmosphere of HD 189733 b. svs.gsfc.nasa.gov

Instrumentation

SISTINE

The SISTINE sounding rocket payload is a far-ultraviolet imaging spectrograph designed to observe nearby exoplanet-hosting stars. UV radiation plays a key role in the formation, evolution, and loss of planetary atmospheres and SISTINE has been a trailblazer for future UV observatories by testing state of the art UV optics and detectors.

In 2022, we took SISTINE to the Northern Territory of Australia for its third successful launch to observe Alpha Centauri! See a follow-along documentary from NASA Goddard: High Above Down Under

FLUID

The FLUID sounding rocket payload is a far- and Lyman-ultraviolet imaging telescope. FLUID uses four unique narrow band filters in the UV to distinguish O- and B-type stars and to observe large-scale galaxy morphology.

Observation

My research uses observations of stars and exoplanets from the Hubble Space Telescope, Chandra X-Ray Observatory, and XMM-Newton. I am part of the MUSCLES group where we create spectra of exoplanet hosting stars for the exoplanet community to use in atmospheric modeling. Our spectra were used in the first detection of photochemically produced SO2 an exoplanet atmosphere!

MUSCLES Extension for Atmospheric Transmission Spectroscopy

The original MUSCLES survey focused exclusively on M dwarfs; MEATS extends that survey to include include hotter stars up to F4. Spectra of the stars from MEATS can be found on the MUSCLES page on MAST

KELT-20 b: A transiting Ultra-hot Jupiter

KELT-20 b is an ultra-hot Jupiter orbiting a young A2 star. We've been using the HST Cosmic Origins Spectrograph to observe the transit of KELT-20 b in the far-UV! This is the first time an UHJ has been studied in the FUV and the results are bound to be interesting; stay tuned!
